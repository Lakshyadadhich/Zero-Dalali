EQUITY TRADING

Equity trading, also known as stock trading, is the buying and selling of company shares on the stock market. Equity represents ownership in a company, and through equity trading, investors can participate in the financial markets by buying and selling these ownership stakes. The primary objective of equity trading is to generate profits through the appreciation of stock prices or through dividends paid by the company.

FUTURES & OPTIONS

Futures and Options (F&0) trading is a specialized segment of the financial markets that revolves around buying and selling derivative products. Derivatives are financial instruments whose value is derived from an underlying asset, such as stocks, bonds, currencies, or commodities.

MARGIN TRADING

Margin trading is a financial strategy that allows investors to buy a larger quantity of an asset than they could with only their own capital. In margin trading, investors borrow funds to increase their buying power, leveraging their existing capital to potentially amplify both gains and losses. This practice is commonly used in various financial markets, including stocks, futures, and foreign exchange.
